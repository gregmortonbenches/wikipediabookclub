The Wikipedia Book Club (London) is an informal, in-person discussion group based in London, United Kingdom. The club meets fortnightly to read, analyse, and discuss articles from the English-language Wikipedia — focusing on topics of cultural, historical, and scientific interest. Meetings are open to the public and aim to promote critical reading and curiosity.

About

The club began in 2025 as a community initiative to explore Wikipedia articles as shared readings. Each meeting focuses on a selected article — typically a featured or well-written entry — chosen for its narrative quality, historical depth, or relevance to current events.

Members take turns suggesting new articles, with discussions ranging from Roman archaeology and literature to contemporary science and cultural history.

Meetings

Meetings are held every other Thursday evening from 7:00 pm to 9:00 pm. The venue rotates between central London cafés, libraries, and cultural venues. Each session starts with a short summary of the chosen article, followed by open discussion.

How to join

Anyone is welcome to attend the Wikipedia Book Club. Attendance is free, and new members are encouraged to bring an article suggestion. To join or receive meeting updates, email: wikipedia.book.club.london@gmail.com.
